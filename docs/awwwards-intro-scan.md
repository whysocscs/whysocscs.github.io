## Takeaways

- Strong intros are usually not busy. They rely on one confident image, one typography system, or one spatial idea.
- Decorative HUD rings and obvious spotlight beams quickly feel like template motion graphics.
- If text must be sharp, keep it in HTML/CSS instead of baking it into a compressed video.
- Video/WebGL should establish atmosphere, not compete with the main hero copy.
- The best first screens use restraint: fewer objects, stronger contrast, cleaner edges, and faster commitment to the actual page.

## Decision For This Portfolio

- Keep the Remotion intro as an atmospheric water transition only.
- Remove the circular splash ring.
- Remove top spotlight beams.
- Remove `Lee Sangho` text from the rendered video so the real page typography stays crisp.
- Let the actual home hero carry the identity after the plunge finishes.
